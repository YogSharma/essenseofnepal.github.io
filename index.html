Top Rated

Pashupati Nath

Pashupati is an ancient Hindu temple complex in Kathmandu, Nepal, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is a revered pilgrimage site, attracting devotees from all over the world to participate in sacred rituals and ceremonies. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Pashupati holds immense cultural and religious significance, embodying the essence of Nepal's rich spiritual heritage.

Muktinath

Muktinath is a sacred pilgrimage site in Mustang, Nepal, revered by Hindus and Buddhists. The temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu (as Muktinath) for Hindus and Avalokiteshvara (as Chenrezig) for Buddhists. Surrounded by stunning Himalayan landscapes, Muktinath attracts pilgrims and trekkers seeking spiritual blessings and enlightenment.

Chitwan National Park

Chitwan a cherished destination in Nepal, known for its rich wildlife and lush landscapes. The Chitwan National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers thrilling wildlife safaris, attracting visitors from around the globe. Explore the diverse flora and fauna, including endangered species like the Bengal tiger and one-horned rhino, making Chitwan a paradise.

Few Lake Pokhara

Fewa Lake, also known as Phewa Lake, is a picturesque freshwater lake situated in the Pokhara Valley of Nepal. It is the second-largest lake in the country and a popular destination for both tourists and locals alike. Surrounded by lush hills and the snow-capped Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges, Fewa Lake offers stunning views and a serene atmosphere.

Popular Destinations

Swayambhu Stupa

Swayambhunath Stupa, also known as the "Monkey Temple," is an ancient and sacred stupa perched atop a hill in Kathmandu, Nepal. This iconic structure is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and holds great religious significance for both Buddhists and Hindus. With its serene ambiance, captivating architecture, and panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley, Swayambhunath Stupa attracts visitors seeking spiritual solace and a glimpse into Nepal's rich cultural heritage.

Sarangkot View Point

Sarangkot View Point is a popular vantage point located near Pokhara, Nepal, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges. Renowned for its stunning sunrise and sunset vistas, Sarangkot is a favorite destination for nature enthusiasts, trekkers, and photographers. Visitors can witness the majestic Himalayan peaks, Phewa Lake, and the surrounding lush landscapes, creating a memorable and awe-inspiring experience in the heart of Nepal's natural beauty.

Lumbini

Lumbini is a sacred and historic site in Nepal, revered as the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became known as Buddha, the founder of Buddhism. It holds immense cultural and spiritual significance for Buddhists worldwide, attracting pilgrims and visitors seeking enlightenment and tranquility. The UNESCO World Heritage Site is adorned with monasteries, stupas, and the Mayadevi Temple, offering a serene and contemplative ambiance for those exploring the roots of Buddhism and the life of Lord Buddha.

Rara Lake

Rara Lake is a pristine and captivating gem nestled in the remote Mugu district of Nepal. As the country's largest lake, Rara boasts crystal-clear waters reflecting the majestic snow-capped peaks surrounding it. With its untouched wilderness and diverse flora and fauna, including endangered species like the red panda, Rara Lake is a paradise for nature lovers and trekkers seeking a serene and off-the-beaten-path adventure in the lap of the Himalayas.
